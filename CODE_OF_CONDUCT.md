# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

JunkedOut is committed to providing a professional, respectful, and inclusive environment for everyone who participates in the project, including contributors, reviewers, maintainers, researchers, moderators, and observers.

Because JunkedOut addresses sensitive topics such as hiring practices, discrimination, and systemic exclusion, we place particular emphasis on evidence-based discussion, restraint, and responsible communication.

Participation in this project implies agreement to abide by this Code of Conduct.

---

## Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

- Treat others with dignity, respect, and fairness.
- Engage in discussions based on evidence, reasoned argument, and good faith.
- Be mindful of the sensitive nature of recruitment, employment, and personal experience.
- Respect differences in background, expertise, culture, and perspective.
- Accept constructive feedback and engage in professional disagreement.
- Follow documented project processes and governance rules.

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated:

- Harassment, intimidation, or threats of any kind.
- Discriminatory, derogatory, or demeaning language or conduct.
- Personal attacks, insults, or character judgments.
- Publishing or attempting to obtain personal or sensitive data about others.
- Brigading, coordinated pressure campaigns, or attempts to manipulate discussion or outcomes.
- Bad-faith participation, including deliberate misrepresentation or obstruction.
- Retaliation against individuals who report concerns or violations.
- Attempts to bypass moderation, governance, or security processes.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project-related spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, and discussions.
- Project documentation and communication channels.
- Community forums, working groups, and collaboration tools.
- Any public or private communication conducted in an official project capacity.

---

## Enforcement

JunkedOut operates under a **founder-led governance model**.

Enforcement decisions are based on documented evidence, proportionality, and the project’s mission and safety requirements.

Possible enforcement actions include:

- Informal warning or clarification request.
- Temporary restriction from specific project spaces.
- Rejection or rollback of contributions.
- Suspension or permanent exclusion from participation.
- Escalation to platform providers or legal authorities when required by law or safety concerns.

All enforcement actions are documented internally. Severe or repeated violations may result in permanent removal.

---

## Reporting Concerns

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it promptly.

### Reporting channels:

- **General concerns:** contact@junkedout.org  
- **Security or sensitive issues:** security@junkedout.org  

Reports should include, where possible:
- A description of the incident.
- Relevant context (links, timestamps, screenshots).
- Any steps already taken.

All reports are handled confidentially and reviewed in good faith.

---

## Protection Against Retaliation

Retaliation against individuals who report concerns or participate in investigations is strictly prohibited and will be treated as a serious violation of this Code of Conduct.

---

## Interpretation and Amendments

This Code of Conduct is interpreted in alignment with:

- The project’s mission and principles.
- The governance framework described in [MANIFESTO.md](./MANIFESTO.md).
- Applicable legal and ethical standards.

The Code of Conduct may be updated as the project evolves. Substantive changes are documented and publicly recorded.

---

## Acknowledgement

This Code of Conduct is inspired by best practices from established open-source communities, adapted to the specific context and responsibilities of a transparency and public-interest platform.

By participating in JunkedOut, you agree to uphold these standards.
